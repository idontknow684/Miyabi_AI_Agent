## Executive Summary

The Miyabi community is built on **mathematical rigor, open collaboration, and respectful discourse**. These guidelines ensure our community embodies our brand values while welcoming developers, researchers, and enterprises who want to verify, contribute, and build on Autonomous Development Systems.

**Core Principle**: "We prove, we share, we improve - together."

---

## 1. Community Values

### Value 1: Verification Over Trust
**What it means**: Every claim must be verifiable. We don't ask members to trust - we provide proofs, code, and data.

**In practice**:
- All technical claims link to proofs or benchmarks
- Community members can (and should) challenge claims with evidence
- "Don't trust, verify" is celebrated, not discouraged

**Community Behavior**:
‚úÖ "I verified Theorem 5.1 in Coq, here's my proof script"
‚úÖ "Reproduced SWE-bench results, got 94.2% (within margin of error)"
‚úÖ "Question: How does SWML handle [edge case]? Here's my analysis"
‚ùå "Just trust that it works"
‚ùå "Miyabi is the best because I say so"

---

### Value 2: Rigor With Kindness
**What it means**: High standards for technical accuracy, high empathy for people learning.

**In practice**:
- Critique ideas rigorously, never people
- Beginners get patient explanations (no "RTFM")
- Mathematical precision + human warmth

**Community Behavior**:
‚úÖ "Your proof has a gap in Step 3. Here's why: [explanation]"
‚úÖ "Great question! Let me break down Theorem 5.1 in simpler terms..."
‚úÖ "I disagree with this approach because [technical reason], but I appreciate you thinking about this"
‚ùå "This is stupid, did you even read the paper?"
‚ùå "RTFM noob"
‚ùå "Obviously you don't understand category theory"

---

### Value 3: Open Collaboration
**What it means**: Research and implementation are open-source. Contributions are celebrated.

**In practice**:
- GitHub is the source of truth
- All papers publicly available (arXiv)
- Community contributions credited prominently
- Forks and alternatives encouraged (if rigorous)

**Community Behavior**:
‚úÖ "I implemented SWML in Python, here's the repo"
‚úÖ "Found a bug in the executor, PR submitted"
‚úÖ "Extended Theorem 5.1 to cover [new case], draft paper: [link]"
‚ùå "This is proprietary, I won't share"
‚ùå Taking credit for others' work
‚ùå Gatekeeping ("only we can work on this")

---

### Value 4: Substance Over Hype
**What it means**: We value quiet excellence over loud mediocrity. Results speak louder than marketing.

**In practice**:
- Data > Anecdotes
- Proofs > Claims
- Working code > Vaporware demos

**Community Behavior**:
‚úÖ "Here are my benchmark results: [data, methodology, repo]"
‚úÖ "I proved this property holds under assumptions X, Y, Z: [proof]"
‚úÖ "Deployed in production, here's what I learned: [lessons]"
‚ùå "This will revolutionize everything!" (without evidence)
‚ùå "Coming soon" announcements without code
‚ùå Hype-driven claims without reproducibility

---

### Value 5: Diversity of Thought, Unity of Purpose
**What it means**: Multiple approaches to autonomous systems are welcome. Shared commitment to mathematical rigor.

**In practice**:
- Different implementations (Rust, Python, Haskell) encouraged
- Alternative theorems/proofs welcomed (if rigorous)
- Respectful debate on approaches
- Zero tolerance for discrimination

**Community Behavior**:
‚úÖ "I disagree with using category theory, here's an alternative: [paper]"
‚úÖ "Implemented SWML in Haskell for different type guarantees"
‚úÖ "Compared Miyabi vs [other approach], here's data: [link]"
‚ùå Dismissing entire fields/approaches without technical justification
‚ùå Discrimination based on identity, background, affiliation
‚ùå "My way or the highway" attitudes

## 2. Code of Conduct

### The Golden Rule
**"Critique ideas rigorously. Treat people kindly."**

### Encouraged Behaviors

**Technical**:
- Rigorous peer review (with constructive feedback)
- Sharing reproductions, benchmarks, proofs
- Asking clarifying questions
- Admitting when you don't know something
- Updating your position when presented with evidence

**Social**:
- Welcoming newcomers
- Crediting others' contributions
- Assuming good intent
- Apologizing when wrong
- Celebrating community wins

---

### Prohibited Behaviors

**Absolute No-Tolerance**:
- Harassment, discrimination, hate speech
- Personal attacks, ad hominem arguments
- Doxxing, threats, intimidation
- Plagiarism, taking credit for others' work
- Spamming, trolling, bad-faith participation

**Discouraged** (warnings before moderation):
- Unconstructive criticism ("this sucks" without explanation)
- Jargon gatekeeping ("you wouldn't understand")
- Hype without substance
- Off-topic political debates
- Excessive self-promotion

---

### Enforcement

**Three-Strike System**:
1. **Warning**: Moderator explains issue, provides guidance
2. **Temporary Ban**: 7-day suspension from community platforms
3. **Permanent Ban**: Removal from all Miyabi community spaces

**Appeals**: Email community@miyabi.dev with evidence

**Moderators**: [List core team members]

## 3. Community Spaces

### GitHub (github.com/miyabi-dev)

**Purpose**: Code collaboration, technical discussions, issue tracking

**Guidelines**:
- **Issues**: Provide reproduction steps, environment details, minimal examples
- **Pull Requests**: Include tests, follow style guide, reference issues
- **Discussions**: Technical questions, feature proposals, research topics
- **Respect**: maintainer time (avoid "when will this be merged?" badgering)

**Tone**: Professional, technical, concise

**Templates**: Use issue/PR templates (when available)

## 9. Conflict Resolution

### Disagreement Protocol

**Step 1: Direct Communication**
- Address the person directly (DM or public, their choice)
- Focus on the technical issue, not the person
- Provide evidence for your position

**Step 2: Moderator Mediation**
- If unresolved, tag a moderator
- Moderator facilitates discussion
- Goal: Understanding, not winning

**Step 3: Decision**
- For technical issues: Defer to core team after community input
- For community issues: Moderators decide based on CoC
- Document rationale publicly

**Principle**: "Disagree and commit" - once decided, everyone moves forward

---

### Reporting Violations

**How to Report**:
- Email: community@miyabi.dev
- Discord DM: Any moderator
- Anonymous form: [link when set up]

**What to Include**:
- Description of violation
- Screenshots/evidence (if available)
- Your perspective (optional)

**What Happens Next**:
- 24-hour acknowledgment
- Investigation (1-7 days)
- Decision communicated to reporter and violator
- Appeal process available

**Confidentiality**: Reporter identity kept confidential unless permission given
