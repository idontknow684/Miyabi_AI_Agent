### Speaking Prep: Media Training Talking Points

**Interview Question 1: "What makes Miyabi different from GitHub Copilot or Devin?"**

**Answer (30 seconds)**:
"Copilot and Devin are built on pattern matching and heuristics. They work well in many cases, but they can't guarantee convergence to your goal.

Miyabi is different because we start with mathematics. We've published formal proofs - Theorem 5.1 specifically - that guarantee our system converges to the desired outcome.

Think of it this way: Copilot suggests code. Devin attempts tasks. Miyabi proves outcomes. That's the difference between statistics and certainty."

---

**Interview Question 2: "Isn't formal verification too slow for modern development?"**

**Answer (30 seconds)**:
"Great question. There's a misconception that rigor means slow.

Our benchmarks show 2.78√ó speedup through parallel execution. We achieve this because mathematical foundations enable better optimization - we know exactly what's safe to parallelize.

The real slowness is debugging heuristic failures. When you have proofs, you debug less. Net result: faster development with higher confidence."

---

**Interview Question 3: "Are you trying to replace developers?"**

**Answer (20 seconds)**:
"Absolutely not. Miyabi amplifies human creativity by handling verified implementation.

Developers focus on what they're best at - solving novel problems, architectural decisions, strategic thinking. Miyabi handles the proven patterns with mathematical guarantees.

It's augmentation, not replacement."

---

**Interview Question 4: "Why did you choose to work independently instead of joining a big lab?"**

**Answer (25 seconds)**:
"Freedom to follow the math wherever it leads.

In corporate research, there's pressure to publish fast or align with product roadmaps. I needed time to get the foundations right - to prove theorems, not just ship demos.

Independence gave me that space. And Claude as a research partner gave me the collaboration I needed."

---

**Interview Question 5: "What's your advice for other researcher-founders?"**

**Answer (30 seconds)**:
"Three things:

One: Don't compromise on rigor. If you can't prove it, don't ship it.

Two: Make complexity accessible. Your theorems mean nothing if no one understands them. Find the metaphors - like 'flickering scenery.'

Three: Ship alongside research. Papers establish credibility. Products create impact. Do both."
