## 1. Brand Story & Narrative

### The Origin Story

**The Paradox**

In 2024, while AI coding assistants proliferated, a fundamental problem remained unsolved: **How do you trust a system you can't predict?**

GitHub Copilot completes your code. Devin builds features. But none can answer the question that keeps CTOs awake at night:

**"Will it work? Will it converge? Can I stake my business on it?"**

### The Insight

Shunsuke Hayashi, an independent researcher in Tokyo, saw what others missed. The problem wasn't technology - it was **mathematics**.

Every autonomous system claimed to "learn" and "improve." But without formal proofs, these were just heuristics dressed in marketing speak.

Drawing from category theory, world models, and convergence theory, Shunsuke formulated a radical hypothesis:

**"What if autonomous development could be proven, not just promised?"**

### The Breakthrough

**Two Papers, One Vision:**

1. **"The Law of Flickering Scenery"** - A poetic metaphor that captures how agents perceive and transform reality through discrete "blinks" - momentary snapshots that accumulate into continuous transformation.

2. **"Shunsuke's World Model Logic (SWML)"** - The mathematical foundation: 5 axioms, category theory, formal convergence proofs. The first complete logical system for autonomous agent behavior.

Together, they represent the duality at Miyabi's core:
- **Art** and **Science**
- **Intuition** and **Rigor**
- **Poetry** and **Proof**

### The Name: Miyabi (ÈõÖ)

**Meaning**: Elegance, refinement, courtly grace (Japanese aesthetic concept)

**Why It Works**:
- **Memorable**: Distinct from generic AI names
- **Meaningful**: Reflects our philosophy - elegant mathematics, refined engineering
- **Cultural**: Bridges Japan (innovation hub) and global markets
- **Pronounceable**: Works in all languages (mee-YAH-bee)
- **Ownable**: Unique in tech space

**Etymology Connection**:
Just as Miyabi in classical Japanese culture represented the highest form of aesthetic refinement - where every element served a purpose without excess - our system achieves **mathematical elegance**. Every transformation, every blink, every axiom serves the goal of provable convergence.

### The Mission We're On

**Short-term (2025-2027)**:
Prove that mathematically grounded autonomous development isn't just possible - it's superior. Replace heuristics with theorems. Replace hope with certainty.

**Medium-term (2027-2030)**:
Become the infrastructure layer for autonomous software development. Just as NVIDIA powers AI training, Miyabi powers AI development.

**Long-term (2030+)**:
Redefine what it means to create software. A world where human developers focus on creativity and strategy while Miyabi handles implementation with mathematical guarantees.

### Core Values

#### 1. Mathematical Rigor Above All
**What it means**: Every claim must be provable. Every system must have formal guarantees. No marketing without math.

**In practice**:
- Publish proofs alongside features
- Open-source verification tools
- Academic partnerships for validation
- "No Heuristics" badge for certified components

**Story**: When a competitor claimed "98% accuracy," we responded with Theorem 5.1: Convergence Guarantee. They had statistics. We had certainty.

#### 2. Poetic Simplicity
**What it means**: Complex mathematics must be communicated through elegant metaphors. Accessibility without dilution.

**In practice**:
- "Flickering Scenery" over "Iterative State Transformation Algorithm"
- Visual storytelling (blinks, convergence animations)
- Layered documentation (metaphor ‚Üí intuition ‚Üí math)

**Story**: Our best marketing material is a 24fps animation of a world transforming through blinks. Developers get it in 30 seconds.

#### 3. Transparency as Trust
**What it means**: Show the work. Open-source the core. Make verification reproducible.

**In practice**:
- All theorems published on arXiv
- Reference implementation fully public
- Benchmarks independently verifiable
- "Glass Box AI" - no black boxes

**Story**: When asked "How do we know it works?" We say: "Don't trust us. Verify. Here's the proof, the code, and the test suite."

#### 4. Developer-First, Always
**What it means**: Build for the developer who codes, not the executive who signs checks. (They follow where developers lead.)

**In practice**:
- CLI before GUI
- Documentation before sales decks
- GitHub before G2 Crowd
- Community before campaigns

**Story**: Our product roadmap comes from GitHub issues, not board meetings.

#### 5. Ambition Grounded in Reality
**What it means**: Chase moonshots, but show the math. Vision inspires, proofs deliver.

**In practice**:
- "We aim to replace 78% of repetitive development" (backed by SWE-bench)
- Not: "We'll replace all developers" (absurd)
- Incremental milestones with exponential impact

**Story**: We say "mathematical guarantees for bounded problems" not "AGI for coding."

## 3. Brand Personality

### Personality Framework: The Sage-Innovator

**Primary Archetype: The Sage** (60%)
- Wisdom through deep knowledge
- Truth-seeking, verification-obsessed
- Trusted advisor, not flashy disruptor

**Secondary Archetype: The Innovator** (40%)
- Pushing boundaries of what's possible
- First-mover, paradigm-shifter
- Bold but backed by substance

### Tone of Voice

#### Dimensions:

**Formal ‚Üî Casual**: 70% Formal (Academic authority with accessible moments)

**Respectful ‚Üî Irreverent**: 80% Respectful (Confident without arrogance)

**Enthusiastic ‚Üî Matter-of-fact**: 60% Matter-of-fact (Let the math speak)

**Serious ‚Üî Funny**: 85% Serious (Occasional dry wit)

#### Voice Characteristics:

**We are**:
- Precise (like our theorems)
- Confident (because we have proofs)
- Accessible (complex ideas, simple words)
- Humble (about limitations, transparent about constraints)

**We are NOT**:
- Hype-driven
- Jargon-heavy without explanation
- Dismissive of non-technical audiences
- Apologetic about being academic

#### Examples:

**Good**:
- "Theorem 5.1 proves convergence. No asterisks. No 'usually.' Just math."
- "Most AI tools promise reliability. We prove it. There's a difference."
- "94.7% goal achievement isn't marketing. It's measurement."

**Bad**:
- "Revolutionary AI that will change everything!" (Vague, hype)
- "Utilizing advanced ML paradigms to synergize..." (Jargon salad)
- "We're pretty good at this, maybe check it out?" (Weak, uncertain)

### Communication Style by Channel

**Academic Papers**: Full rigor, formal notation, peer-review ready
**Blog Posts**: Accessible metaphors ‚Üí intuitive explanations ‚Üí mathematical deep-dives (layered)
**Twitter/X**: Concise, punchy, confident. Show, don't tell (demos, visuals)
**Sales Materials**: ROI-focused, proof-backed, executive-friendly
**Developer Docs**: Code-first, example-rich, assume competence

## 9. Brand Guidelines: Dos and Don'ts

### Do:
- Start with the problem, end with the proof
- Use mathematical notation correctly (LaTeX-rendered)
- Show benchmarks with methodology
- Link to papers, code, proofs
- Use "blink" and "convergence" as brand vocabulary
- Explain complexity in layers (metaphor ‚Üí intuition ‚Üí math)
- Admit limitations transparently
- Challenge competitors on rigor, not marketing

### Don't:
- Make claims without proofs
- Use "AI" without context (be specific: autonomous agents, formal systems)
- Overpromise on timelines (under-promise, over-deliver)
- Dismiss non-technical audiences (translate, don't condescend)
- Rely on hype (we have substance)
- Compare using vanity metrics (compare mathematical foundations)
- Use jargon without explanation
- Pretend limitations don't exist
