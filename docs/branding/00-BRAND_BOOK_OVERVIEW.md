## Executive Summary

This Brand Book provides the complete branding and positioning strategy for Miyabi - the world's first mathematically proven autonomous development system. Built on the foundation of two peer-reviewed research papers ("The Law of Flickering Scenery" and "SWML"), Miyabi is creating the "Autonomous Development Systems" category and positioning for $10-30M Series A fundraising within 18-24 months.

**Core Strategy**: Research-first credibility ‚Üí Developer community ‚Üí Enterprise adoption ‚Üí Venture funding

**Inspired by**: Sakana AI ($244M raised in 8 months, $1.5B valuation)

### Brand Differentiation Framework

**What competitors say**: "Our AI helps you code faster"
**What Miyabi says**: "We prove your task will converge. Mathematically."

**The Difference**:
- **Competitors**: Heuristics, patterns, statistics ("probably works")
- **Miyabi**: Theorems, proofs, guarantees ("provably works")

**The Moat**:
- Peer-reviewed research (2 papers)
- Formal proofs (Theorem 5.1)
- Open-source verification
- Academic credibility

**The Result**:
- Competitors can copy features ‚Üí We can copy features
- Competitors cannot copy theorems ‚Üí We have research moat
- Trust built on mathematics, not marketing
